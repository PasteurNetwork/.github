# 🌍 Pasteur Network
> "Science knows no countries." — Louis Pasteur

## About Us
The Pasteur Network is an alliance of 32 organisations which plays a crucial role in tackling global health challenges through science, innovation and public health. Its distinctive strength lies in the diversity and extensive geographic reach of its membership, spanning 25 countries across five continents and united by shared values and missions for the benefit of populations, fostering a dynamic community of scientific knowledge and expertise that is both locally embedded and globally connected 

The Pasteur Network is recognized as a WHO non-state actor, and members of the network are frequently embedded into local Ministries of Health. The PN sustains a global infrastructure encompassing 50+ national and regional reference laboratories, which includes multiple Biosafety Level 3 Laboratories, and 17 WHO Collaborating Centers.   
Its work is guided by four strategic pillars:

- 🦠 **Epidemic & pandemic intelligence and preparedness**, with a focus on climate-sensitive infectious diseases  
- 🔬 **Research, development, and innovation**  
- 🤝 **Knowledge communities** — fostering multi-disciplinary, regional, and global collaboration  
- ⚖️ **Good governance and equity**

🔗 Learn more at [pasteur-network.org](https://pasteur-network.org)

## What's Hosted Here
This GitHub organisation will serve as a **shared open-source hub** for collaborative projects **initiated and led by YOU**, members of the Pasteur Network. 

Repositories will span the full breadth of our scientific and public health missions, including — but not limited to: Bioinformatics, AI/ML, Biostatistics, GIS, Data Management, Digital Health, 

Each repository remains under the stewardship of its initiating team. The Network serves as a hub — amplifying visibility, facilitating connections with contributors across the global network, and helping projects grow.

## How to Contribute

### 💡 Propose a Project

Any researcher, scientist, engineer, or public health professional affiliated with a **Pasteur Network member organization** can propose an open-source collaborative project to be hosted here.

To propose a project:

1. **Open a Discussion** in this organisation using the *Project Idea* template.  
2. Describe your project: its scientific scope, the problem it addresses, a list of your initial team to be added as repository maintainers, the type of contributors you are looking for, and the expected outputs.  
3. The Network team will review your proposal and, once validated, create a dedicated repository under the organisation.

**You keep the lead.** The original proposer and their team remain the **repository maintainer(s)**. The Pasteur Network organisation serves as a discovery and collaboration hub — not a governing body over your project.

### 🤝 Join an Existing Project

Browse the repositories to find projects where your skills can make a difference. Each repository includes a `CONTRIBUTING.md` guide with instructions specific to that project. 

Some ways to contribute:

- 🐛 Report bugs or suggest improvements  
- 🔧 Submit pull requests (code, documentation, tests)  
- 🌐 Help with translations or localisation  
- 📖 Improve documentation and READMEs  
- 🔍 Review and provide feedback on open pull requests

**Note**: Each repository is led and managed by their own maintainers. The Pasteur Network organisation only serves as a discovery and collaboration hub.
